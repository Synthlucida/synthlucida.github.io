Relax Player: Crossfade, Playback History & Your Own Playlist

Tracks no longer have to end abruptly. A new CROSSFADE control lets you choose 3, 6, 8, or 10 seconds of smooth blending between the end of one track and the start of the next, or turn it off entirely for a hard cut. It plays nicely with both shuffle and repeat, so the transition always leads into the track you'd expect.

There's also a new 🕘 HISTORY panel that keeps track of the last tracks you've listened to, across every playlist, along with when you played them. Tap a track name to jump straight back into it, or clear the whole list whenever you'd like a fresh start.

And now you can build something of your own: hit the + next to any track in HISTORY to send it to 🎵 MY PLAYLIST, reorder the list with ▲▼, remove anything with ✕, then press PLAY to listen through it in exactly the order you put it together — shuffle, repeat, and crossfade all still work right alongside it.
July 17, 2026

Relax Player: Lock Screen Controls, Keyboard Shortcuts & Smarter Playback

The Relax Player now integrates with your device's lock screen and notification media controls — play, pause, skip to the next or previous track, and scrub through the current one, all without unlocking your phone or switching back to the browser tab. The current track's title and artwork now show up right there too.

On desktop, playback can also be controlled entirely from the keyboard: Space toggles play/pause, the left and right arrows jump back or forward 10 seconds, and the up/down arrows adjust the volume.

Playback is a bit smarter as well. If a track fails to load — a dead link or a brief network hiccup — the player now automatically skips to the next one instead of silently stalling with no sound. And the last track you were listening to, along with its exact position, is remembered between visits, so pressing play picks up right where you left off.

Relax Player: Audio Crackling Fix on Mobile

A few days ago a limiter (DynamicsCompressor) was added to stop digital clipping when several sound layers stack up past 0dBFS — that part was already working fine. But some phones, notably the Xiaomi Redmi Note 15 Pro+ 5G, kept crackling occasionally even after that fix, while the same session ran clean on a PC or a Motorola. It turned out to be a completely different issue: a buffer underrun, where the phone's audio thread can't recompute the next block of samples in time, leaving an audible gap or click. A limiter can't fix that, since it's a performance problem, not a loudness one — and HyperOS/MIUI is known for handling background audio scheduling less consistently than closer-to-stock Android, even on a chip that's stronger on paper.

Two real sources of unnecessary load were found in the code. The reverb (convolution) was running constantly even when switched off — every note and kick was still being sent through a 3.5-second convolution impulse regardless of the toggle, with the volume only zeroed out afterward. And the AudioContext wasn't given a latency hint, so the browser defaulted to the smallest, most sensitive buffer size, leaving almost no room for one slow frame before it became audible.

What changed: the AudioContext now requests a "playback" latency hint for a larger internal buffer and much more headroom against the odd slow frame (latency goes up slightly, imperceptible on ambient pads); reverb is only routed into the mix when it's actually turned on, so switching it off now truly costs nothing; and the reverb's impulse response has been shortened from 3.5s to 2.5s — still a long, lush tail, just a bit lighter to compute when reverb is on.

Canvas: A Relaxing Painting Update

The drawing Canvas just got a lot more relaxing. You can now pick a BRUSH FEEL — SOFT for a gentle watercolor wash, INK for a tapered brush-pen stroke, CHALK for a dry pastel texture, or the original glowing NEON — alongside a new set of COLORING PAGE outlines (mandala, flower, butterfly and more) that you can simply paint inside like a real coloring book. Bring To Life also has new gentle motions — PULSE, FADE, DRIFT, and TWINKLE — for softer, breathing animations. And for the first time, Canvas plays quiet ambient tones as you draw, always tuned to a calming five-note scale so they never sound off. Rounding it off, a new calm ZEN theme has joined the background options.
