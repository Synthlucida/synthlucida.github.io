    SYNTHLUCIDA — User Guide

    Experience 24/7 uninterrupted streaming. Enjoy continuous, free listening. Whether you need background music for focus, deep relaxation, or daily activities, this stream provides the perfect atmospheric soundtrack. Find out more at www.SYNTHLUCIDA.com.

    Playback

    Use the center button to play or pause the current track, and ⏭ to skip to the next one. Tap 🔀 to shuffle the playlist or 🔁 to repeat the current track. Drag the progress bar to seek to any point, and use the slider to adjust the volume.

    Atmospheres & playlists

    Switch between the ASMR, PIANO and CHILL playlists at the top of the screen. GAME and RELAX open separate pages with their own soundscapes. The playlist you last listened to is remembered automatically the next time you open the player.

    Visual themes

    Choose from NEON, SPACE, EARTH, SYNTH, DARK, BLOOD, AQUA, GOLD, MATRIX, AMBER, LAVENDER, AURORA, SAKURA, FROST and ROSEGOLD to change the colors and ambient glow of the player. NEON and AURORA shift gently over time on their own.

    Ambient effects

    RAIN overlays a soft falling-rain animation, and SUNSET blends the background into a warm gradient. Both effects can be combined with any visual theme, and are remembered when you save your settings.

    Breathing exercise

    Tap BREATHE to open a full-screen guided breathing session with a slowly pulsing orb. Inside that screen, its own ⓘ icon opens a dedicated guide explaining each breathing technique — 4-7-8, Box, Coherent, Calm and Sigh — in detail.

    Sleep timer

    Set playback to stop automatically after 15, 30 or 60 minutes using the timer buttons, or cancel an active timer at any time with ✖.

    Save & fullscreen

    SAVE stores your current theme, effects and other preferences so they load automatically the next time you visit. FULLSCREEN hides the browser interface for a cleaner, distraction-free view.

    Offline listening

    ⬇ DOWNLOAD OFFLINE saves the current playlist for offline playback. Offline listening works only inside the app, not in a regular browser tab.

    How to breathe

    Sit comfortably and follow the glowing orb: it expands on the inhale, holds steady and shimmers during a hold, and shrinks on the exhale. Breathe through the nose when the technique doesn't say otherwise. Pick whichever technique fits the moment — you can switch anytime.

    4-7-8 — Dr. Weil

    Inhale quietly through the nose for 4s, hold for 7s, then exhale completely through the mouth for 8s. Popularized by Dr. Andrew Weil, this ratio is widely used to ease anxiety and wind down before sleep by shifting the body toward a "rest and digest" state.

    Box — Navy SEAL method

    Inhale for 4s, hold for 4s, exhale for 4s, hold empty for 4s. An equal-count pattern used by Navy SEALs and first responders to stay calm and sharply focused under pressure.

    Coherent breathing

    Inhale and exhale evenly for about 5.5s each — roughly 5-6 breaths per minute. Studied as a resonance frequency in heart-rate variability (HRV) research, linked to better vagal tone and stress resilience over time.

    Calm — extended exhale

    Inhale for 4s, exhale for 6s, making the out-breath longer than the in-breath. A longer exhale stimulates the vagus nerve and slows the heart rate — a quick way to downshift after a stressful moment.

    Sigh — Huberman Lab

    Two inhales through the nose (one long, one short "sip" on top of it), followed by one long, slow exhale through the mouth. Studied at Stanford's Huberman Lab as one of the fastest ways to lower physiological stress in real time.

    General relaxation information only — not medical advice. If you feel dizzy or unwell, breathe normally and stop.
